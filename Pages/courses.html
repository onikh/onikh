CS1 ISP: POKEMIGUEL

Inspired by the likes of Pokemon and Golden Sun, Pokemiguel was the first game I created. The player has to traverse a forest on a secluded island, where they reach the center of the island to fight Miguel, the antagonist of the game (and also a good friend of mine). The game has three distinct phases: the title screen, the overworld, and the boss battle.



The biggest challenge for my project was bounding. There are two coordinate systems in play in the overworld system of my game: the background image, and the bounding rects and other objects onscreen, which adapted to browser size. The issue was that These weren't multiples of each other, meaning that when the image moved 10 pixels the corresponding image would need to move (just as a hypothetical) 8.6, but could only move in integer quantities. As you approached objects, they moved towards the character faster than the image or vice versa, resulting in a desync between the objects and the background. To fix this, I created a function that calculated the distance of an object from the character, and used the browser size to calculate the disparity for each step the character took between the background and the objects. Using this, I can purposely offset the object by it's distance from the character times the truncating remainder of the equivalent unit in the image, meaning that all objects are desynced when you spawn, but as you approach them they sync. This has a margin of error less than 1 pixel, not affecting gameplay. Adapting the game to any screen size was incredibly difficult, and I had to take advantage of functions that returned the size of the canvas.
